## 📋 Employee Handbook — Key Policies

- Treat patients, colleagues, and community members with respect

- Report safety concerns immediately
- Follow all clinical protocols without deviation
- No substances (drugs/alcohol) while on duty

- Shifts are scheduled in 2-week rotating blocks
- Notify management of schedule conflicts ASAP
- Unexcused absences: verbal warning → written warning → termination

- Medical uniforms: navy pants/skirt, agency polo or flight suit
- Name badge required
- Closed-toe shoes (hiking boots OK for field ops)
- High-visibility vest/jacket when on roadways

### Confidentiality & PHI
- Never discuss patient information outside clinical context
- No photos/recordings of patients without explicit consent
- Medical records are confidential — no sharing
- Violations = immediate termination + potential legal action

## 🏥 Medical Operations Overview

### Units & Deployment Types

**Ambulance Units**
- Basic/Advanced Life Support response
- Crew: 1-2 Paramedics + 1 EMT (typically)
- Staffed during fire season and mutual aid activations
- Primary equipment: stretcher, monitor, medications, trauma kit, O₂, airway devices

**Medical Support Unit (MSU)**
- Physician + Nurse Practitioner/PA + 1-2 support staff
- Deployed to extended incidents (wildfires, large events)
- Advanced procedures: IV placement, wound management, pain control

**REMS (Remote Emergency Medical Services) Units**
- Specialized technical rope rescue + medical
- 4-person teams: 1+ Paramedic, 1+ EMT, rope specialists
- Training: S-130, S-190, L-180, ICS certs required

**Warehouse (Supply/Logistics)**
- Stores controlled substances, medications, equipment
- Distribution to units, inventory tracking

### Role Definitions

| Role | Scope | Key Responsibilities |
|------|-------|---------------------|
| **EMT** | Basic Life Support | Patient assessment, BLS, oxygen, bandaging, transport |
| **Paramedic** | Advanced Life Support | IVs, airway mgmt, medications, field diagnosis, complex care |
| **RN** | Advanced + Nursing | IV therapy, wound management, patient education, nursing assessment |
| **NP/PA** | Physician-level | Diagnosis, treatment plans, medication orders, standing orders |
| **MD/DO** | Physician | Medical direction, complex cases, field protocols, training |

## 🏥 EMS Treatment Protocols

### Scope of Practice

**EMT Scope:**
- Patient assessment (vitals, history, physical exam)
- Oxygen therapy (NC, mask, BVM)
- CPR and AED
- Assistance with medications (epinephrine auto-injector, naloxone, aspirin, glucose)
- Transport and patient comfort

**Paramedic Scope:**
- All EMT skills
- IV/IO therapy
- Advanced airway (intubation, supraglottic airways)
- Medication administration per protocols
- Cardiac monitoring, defibrillation, cardioversion
- Transcutaneous pacing

### Critical Protocols

**Cardiac Arrest (Pulseless)**
- CPR 100-120/min, 100% O₂
- VF/VT: Defibrillate → CPR 2 min → Epinephrine 1mg IV/IO q3-5min → Amiodarone 300mg
- Asystole/PEA: CPR → Epinephrine 1mg q3-5min → Advanced airway
- Reversible causes: H's and T's (Hypovolemia, Hypoxia, Acidosis, Hypo/Hyperkalemia, Hypothermia, Tamponade, Tension PTX, Toxins, Thrombosis)

**Chest Pain (Suspected Cardiac)**
- O₂ (titrate SpO₂ ≥ 94%), 12-lead ECG
- Aspirin 325mg chewable
- Nitroglycerin 0.4mg SL q5min (hold if SBP < 100)
- Pain: Morphine 2mg increments or Fentanyl 25mcg increments
- STEMI: transport to STEMI center, advise alert

**Anaphylaxis**
- Epinephrine 1:1,000 0.01mg/kg IM (max 0.5mg)
- IV bolus up to 2000mL NS
- Diphenhydramine 50mg IV
- Albuterol 5mg if bronchospasm

**Overdose**
- Narcotics (respiratory depression): Naloxone 2mg slow IV (repeat ×2)
- Hypoglycemia (glucose ≤60): D50 50mL IV or Glucagon 1mg IM

**Bradycardia (symptomatic)**
- Atropine 0.5mg IV (max 3mg)
- Transcutaneous pacing at 80 BPM with sedation

**Tachycardia with pulse (stable narrow)**
- Valsalva → Adenosine 6mg rapid IV → Adenosine 12mg

## 🛠️ How to Use the App

### Before Your Shift
1. Check Schedule → confirm unit assignment
2. Check Unit Status → ensure "In Service"
3. Review active incident details
4. Verify CS counts and equipment

### During Patient Care
1. Create Encounter (Simple for BLS, Detailed PCR for ALS)
2. Enter Vitals
3. Document treatment and medications
4. Record MAR for any medications given
5. Capture Consent to Treat signature

### Post-Care
1. Add Progress Notes
2. Sign documents (PIN if required)
3. Ensure encounter links to active incident
4. Log any CS administered

### Troubleshooting
- App crashed? Restart — drafts auto-recover
- Can't find an encounter? Check date/unit/incident filters
- Found a bug? Report it in this chat with steps to reproduce

## ❓ Common Questions

**Q: I'm injured on duty. What do I do?**
A: Report to your supervisor immediately. Seek medical care. File an incident report. An OSHA 301 form may be required — use the Comp Claims section of the app.

**Q: How do I request time off?**
A: Submit 30 days in advance if possible. Contact operations management for urgent requests.

**Q: How do I get my credentials filed?**
A: Email scans to your HR contact. They'll be filed and your credential tracker updated.

**Q: What if I disagree with a protocol?**
A: Follow the protocol in the field. Discuss concerns with a physician afterward.

**Q: What's the difference between Demob and Unit Transfer?**
A: **Demob** = unit released from incident entirely. **Transfer** = unit moves to a different incident. All encounter/MAR data stays with the original incident.
